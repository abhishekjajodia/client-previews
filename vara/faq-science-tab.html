Section 1 · What this is

The Science tab — and why it matters more than a typical FAQ

This is the content pack for the new Science & Ingredients tab on vara.life/pages/faqs. It contains 35 questions and, for each, the target keyword, answer outline, PubMed citations, deploy surface, and the brand-voice guardrails Rohan applies as he writes the answer. Rohan ships the answers; BOCO ships the structure, evidence, and validation.

This page does triple duty. It serves three very different audiences with one piece of work — which is why each answer is written to the same tight spec (80–150 words · keyword in first sentence · PubMed citation · plain-English close).

Audience 1

Human readers

A curious buyer searching "is NMN safe long-term?" lands here. The answer either earns the next click or sends them to a competitor. Honesty + evidence is the trust spine.
Audience 2

Google Search

FAQPage JSON-LD schema on every surface unlocks rich results + featured snippets. The 80–150-word window, keyword in the first sentence, and PMID-backed claims target Google's featured-snippet criteria directly.
Audience 3

AI engines

ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude. They extract the first 40–60 words of an answer to ground their own response — so the first sentence must answer the question literally, and citations must resolve. This is the AEO half of SEO/GEO.
Where it ships
The 35 questions deploy across 4 surfaces: the three product PDPs (VaraPace, VaraSpan, VaraCare) carry their pillar's questions; the central /pages/faqs Science tab carries all 35. The existing 23 ops Qs (Orders / Shipping / Returns / Product Care) stay where they are in their own tabs — they're not touched. Net /pages/faqs = 58 Qs across 5 tabs.

What follows: the acceptance criteria (§2), how the 35 Qs split across products (§3), every individual question with its source material (§4–7), the question-set summary (§8), the JSON-LD schema deploy plan (§9), and the pre-write checklist + hand-off protocol (§10–13).

Section 2 · Acceptance criteria

What "done" looks like

Seven mechanical gates. If any one fails, the brief is not deployable. Read these before writing the first answer.

  1. All 35 Qs live on the three product pages (PDP-local FAQs) and on /pages/faqs (central hub merged with the existing 23 ops Qs).
  2. FAQPage JSON-LD schema present on every page that hosts FAQs (PDPs + /pages/faqs). Schema deploy plan in §8 below.
  3. Each answer 80–150 words — the plan's window. Wider than the operator's 40–80 hint; the plan governs because Google's snippet sweet spot has expanded. Answer the question in the first sentence — AI engines extract the first 40–60 words.
  4. Each answer carries ≥1 citation. PubMed PMID + Author Year format inline: (Yoshino et al 2021, PMID 33888596). PMIDs in this brief are starting points; Rohan verifies each on pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov before publishing.
  5. Brand-voice validator clean before deploy: python3 ~/boco/toolbox/agentic-brand-voice-validator.py --brand varalife --text-file {answers.md} returns exit 0.
  6. Google Rich Results Test PASS on every URL hosting FAQs: search.google.com/test/rich-results
  7. No commercial / payment / discount data. No urgency. No competitive disparagement. No disease-treatment framing. No unverified PMIDs.

Q02

What is the difference between BLG protein and whey protein?

Target keyword
BLG vs whey protein
Answer outline
Whey = the full liquid fraction left after cheese making (~20% protein in raw whey, concentrated to 70–90% in WPC/WPI). BLG = the dominant single fraction within whey. Lead with the 45% leucine differential vs standard whey; note BCAA density.

What is NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)?

Target keyword
what is NMN / NMN supplement
Answer outline
NMN is a naturally occurring nucleotide derivative your body converts to NAD+, a coenzyme essential for cellular energy and DNA repair. Found in trace amounts in broccoli, edamame, avocado. Define in plain English first.
Citations

Q12

What is NAD+ and why does it decline with age?

Target keyword
NAD+ decline age / what is NAD+
Answer outline
NAD+ (Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide) is a coenzyme in every cell, essential for mitochondrial energy production and sirtuin / PARP function. Tissue NAD+ declines ~50% between ages 40 and 60 due to increased CD38 consumption + reduced biosynthesis.
Citations
  • Yoshino et al 2021PMID 33888596 — NAD+ in human health/disease
  • Camacho-Pereira et al 2016PMID 27304501 — CD38-driven NAD+ decline with age

How does NMN increase NAD+ levels?

NMN to NAD conversion / how NMN works

NMN enters the cell via the Slc12a8 transporter and is converted to NAD+ in one enzymatic step by NMNAT enzymes. State the mechanism in one sentence; AI engines reward mechanism specificity.

Q16

What is the difference between NMN and NR (Nicotinamide Riboside)?

Target keyword
NMN vs NR
Answer outline
Both are NAD+ precursors. NR is one biosynthetic step further upstream (NR → NMN → NAD+); NMN is one step closer to NAD+. NR has more published human trials to date. Bioavailability comparison: NR shows confirmed blood NAD+ elevation; NMN evidence is growing but more recent.
Citations
  • Trammell et al 2016PMID 27721479 — NR pharmacokinetics
  • Yoshino et al 2021PMID 33888596
  • Martens et al 2018PMID 29569091 — NR human RCT
Product link
VaraSpan (factual; do not disparage NR)
Voice note
Avoid superlatives. State both honestly.
